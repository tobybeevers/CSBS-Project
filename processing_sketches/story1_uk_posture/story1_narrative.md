# Story 1 — Narrative Notes

## Headline
**UK organisations are getting better at managing cyber security — but breaches remain stubbornly high.**

## What the Governance Score Actually Means

The governance score is **not** a self-reported maturity rating. It is a constructed composite index (Score B) averaging three normalised components per respondent:

1. **Board priority** (`priority_norm`) — how highly the organisation rates cyber security at senior level. Derived from a 4-point scale (1=Very high → 4=Very low), normalised to 0–1.
2. **Management practices** (`manage_prop`) — proportion of 4 active management indicators in place (senior ownership, risk assessments, formal policy, training). Score 0–1.
3. **Policy coverage** (`policy_prop`) — proportion of 5 specific policy areas covered. Score 0–1.

A score of **50.5% in 2025** means the average organisation has adopted approximately **half of the maximum possible governance indicators** across those three dimensions. It does not mean 50.5% of organisations consider themselves well-governed.

---

## Interpretation Caveats

### Sample size doubled mid-series
Participant counts grew from ~2,000 in 2018–2022 to ~4,000 by 2023–2025. The apparent rise in governance scores cannot be cleanly separated from changes in who is being surveyed. A larger, more diverse sample capturing more medium and large organisations will naturally produce different aggregate scores.

### Two methodology changes
Flagged at **2021** and **2024** — these are structural breaks in the data. Year-on-year comparisons across these points should be treated with caution.

### 2020 is an outlier context
2020 has the smallest sample (1,900) and produced the highest governance score to that point alongside elevated breach prevalence. COVID-19 affected remote working patterns, cyber threat exposure, and likely survey participation. The 2020 estimates are noisier and have specific contextual factors that are not present in other waves.

### Weighted percentages, not raw counts
All figures use the CSBS `weight` variable to produce nationally representative estimates. Weighting corrects for known sampling imbalances but cannot correct for response bias if certain organisation types were systematically less likely to participate in a given year.

---

## Core Tension for the Narrative

Governance activity (as measured by the index) appears to have increased. Breach prevalence has not fallen — it has remained between 30% and 48% across all 8 waves, peaking at 48% in 2024.

The uncomfortable conclusion: **whatever organisations are doing more of, it is not consistently reducing their exposure to breaches.** This sets up the central question for Stories 2–5 — does it matter *what kind* of governance activity organisations undertake, not just *how much*?
