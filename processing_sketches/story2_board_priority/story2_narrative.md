## What "Board Priority" Actually Means

Board priority (`board_priority_pct`) is the **weighted proportion of organisations that rate cyber security as a "very high" or "fairly high" priority at board/senior management level** — derived from the `priority` variable (Q9), a 4-point scale where 1=Very high and 4=Very low.

A figure of 70.0% for micro businesses in 2025 means that **70 in every 100 micro organisations** report senior leadership considers cyber security to be a high or very high priority. It is a stated priority measure, not a practice or outcome measure.

---

## Why the Slope Chart Shows 2018 vs 2025 Only

The chart uses a slope chart (not a time series) to foreground the net change between the survey endpoints. The full 8-year data is available in the CSV and shows more volatility, particularly at the 2021 and 2023 methodology changes. The 2018-to-2025 comparison gives the cleanest signal over the period as a whole.

## Interpretation Caveats

### Size band definitions are stable, but composition may shift
`sizeb` codes 1–4 (Micro/Small/Medium/Large) are stable across all 8 waves. However, within each band the mix of sectors, industries, and regions may have changed — especially with the sample doubling from ~2,000 to ~4,000 from 2023 onwards. Aggregate shifts within a band could partly reflect who is now being surveyed, not only changes in behaviour.

### Micro businesses are hard to reach
Micro organisations (<10 employees) form a large proportion of UK businesses but are typically underrepresented in surveys relative to their true number. CSBS weighting corrects for known imbalances, but micro respondents are more likely to be atypical — those willing to complete a cybersecurity survey may not represent the broader micro-business population.

### 2021 and 2023 are structural breaks
The drop in Micro from 81.3% (2022) to 69.8% (2023) coincides with the methodology change and sample expansion. This is likely a measurement artefact rather than a genuine 11pp collapse in one year. The 2018-to-2025 comparison deliberately sidesteps this noise.

### Priority ≠ practice
High stated board priority does not mean organisations have implemented policies, controls, or governance practices. Story 1 (governance composite) and Stories 3–5 examine whether stated priority translates into measurable activity and reduced breach exposure.

### 2025 sample sizes per band (approx)
Line thickness in the chart encodes approximate sample size: Micro n≈835, Small n≈457, Medium n≈338, Large n≈220. Micro estimates are the most stable; Large the most volatile.

## Core Tension for the Narrative

Cyber security has become more of a board priority for every size band — **except micro businesses**. The largest organisations have made the most consistent progress (+5.7pp, approaching near-universal board recognition at 97.7%). The smallest businesses have drifted backwards despite a decade of public policy focus on SME cyber security.

This raises the central question for Stories 3–5: **does board priority actually change what organisations do, and does it reduce their exposure to breaches?**
